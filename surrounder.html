Surround·er

SCAVENGER HUNT MOBILE GAME

Surround·er is a scavenger hunt game, that helps bring people together in new environments while showing them fun and interesting facts and useful information about their new school and city.

OVERVIEW

  • ROLE:
    UX/UI Designer
  • OCCASION:
    School Project
  • DURATION:
    July 2019 - MAy 2020 (10 months)
  • METHOD:
    Design Thinking and Iterative Design
  • HOW MIGHT WE...
    Using more interesting way to engage new students and make information and social event more approachable?
  • KEY FEATURES:
    School-base treasure hunt, Avatar Creator

EMPATHY

INITIAL PROBLEM

I use problem statement to help me understand, communicate and build consensus around an initial core project idea. My initial assumption was that there is an opportunity help assimilate new students to a new environment with a focussed gamified application.

Problem Statement

As a new student entering a university in a new city, it can be difficult to meet new people and get to know your surroundings. Currently students tend to use google maps to explore and FB and Line (etc.) to connect with people. These products have some good features but they are too broad and not focussed on what a new student may want or need.

Proposal Solution

Our user need a way to have more physical social activity with other students because they should access useful information, interact with other students and meet new people at the same time easily.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I conduct a COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS process to see what is already in the marketplace. I am looking for what has been done that is successful, what hasn’t worked well and whether there are any gaps in the market that my product can fill.

GeoCaching and Pokeman Go were 2 examples of existing products I researched. I used a traditional SWOT analysis format to present the findings:

Take Away:

  • Free to use without extra cost.
  • Frequent social event with other players in same region.

I also made a business requirement as part of my final analysis I applied the findings to define 4 main areas of consideration for the Surround•er app.

USER RESEARCH

After defining the basic business strategy and core application concept, I now begin to understand users’ needs, goals and frustrations.

For Surround•er I used Survey Monkey to gather specific user information. This work helps me validate my thinking.

I also conduct the user interview with 5 participants and formalised my findings in a presentation that was designed to highlight key learnings from my interviews.

After that, I used affinity map to organise my observations. By doing so, I can organise the user research findings into more visual groupings. This can help me identify relationships between different components of the app.

Take Away:

  • Almost all the interviewees don’t like to go outside alone, but prefer to hang out with friends and do some activities like eating out, exploring places and wandering around.
  • Reason: Lack of motivation, or not sufficient enough to motivate them.
  • 60%of the interviewees don’t mind to play alone, but 80% of the interviewees prefer to play with other players.
  • 60%of the interviewees likes to have some activities to do with other students so that they can familiar with each other.

WRAP UP

It’s my first time using UX and design thinking methods to approach the problem, it’s definitely a learning progress for me as I went through the whole process.

I really enjoyed using pen and paper to sketch the wireframes, especially refining them after talking to the user. Because at that point, I knew that the product is getting better, and I would have more confidence that the product will meet users’ needs.

I would say the most interesting thing I learned from this project is that users won’t always know what they want, so I believe as a UX designer, it is important to find out what users are trying to achieve in a particular circumstance and knowing User's 'Jobs to Be Done'.
