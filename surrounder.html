Surround·er

SCAVENGER HUNT MOBILE GAME

Surround·er is a scavenger hunt game, that helps bring people together in new environments while showing them fun and interesting facts and useful information about their new school and city.

Purpose and Context

After going to the university, I always thought that it would be great if we have some activities or something we can do together with other students to enrich our sense of belongings to the school and the surrounding.

So I decided to design a mobile game app that focuses on the students who just go to the university and hoping to become more familiar with the new environment and knowing new people who can hang out with them together.

 

Objective

Enable students to enjoy social scavenger hunts for virtual objects and locations in their cities.

Showing useful information about their new schools and cities using more fun and interesting way.

 

Process and Insight

 

Approach

APPROACHProblem Statement:

As a new student entering a university in a new city, it can be difficult to meet new people and get to know your surroundings. Currently students tend to use google maps to explore and FB and Line (etc.) to connect with people. These products have some good features but they might be too broad and not focussed on what a new student may want or need.

I use design thinking and iterative design to approach the problem, and define the key point: How might we use more interesting ways to engage new students and make information and social events more approachable?

APPROACHProcess I used:

 

Empathy

EMAPTHYCompetitive Analysis

I conduct a COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS process to see what is already in the marketplace. I am looking for what has been done that is successful, what hasn’t worked well and whether there are any gaps in the market that my product can fill.

GeoCaching and Pokeman Go were 2 examples of existing products I researched. I used a traditional SWOT analysis format to present the findings:

Take Away:

  • Free to use without extra cost.
  • Frequent social event with other players in same region.

EMAPTHYUser Research

After defining the basic business strategy and core application concept, I now begin to understand users’ needs, goals and frustrations.

I used Survey Monkey to gather specific user information, and interviewed with 5 participants.

After that, I used affinity map to organise my observations. By doing so, I can organise the user research findings into more visual groupings. This can help me identify relationships between different components of the app.

Take Away:

  • Almost all the interviewees don’t like to go outside alone, but prefer to hang out with friends and do some activities like eating out, exploring places and wandering around.
  • Reason: Lack of motivation, or not sufficient enough to motivate them to go outside alone.
  • 60% of the interviewees don’t mind to play alone, but 80% of the interviewees prefer to play with other players.
  • 60% of the interviewees likes to have some activities to do with other students so that they can familiar with each other.
 

Define

DEFINEUser Persona

Using PERSONAS helps me empathise with my user. Designing to a specific audience informs my design thinking and ensures better execution.

I have 3 personas that will interact with the Surround·er for differents purposes:

Kelly who wants to meet new friends and knowing the surroundings.

Peter who wants the motivation to go outdoors more.

Vincent who want to play the games with his own friends.

DEFINEUser Journey Map

I then used User Journey Map to visualise processes and emotional motivations a user may have while using the application and making decisions.

It helps me begin to connect the user motivation with specific steps and tasks they will take to accomplish a goal.

Our primary Features should help users’ to achieve:

  • The mobile game that can meet new people easily.
  • Getting to know their surrounding.
  • Motivate the user to go out more even alone.
  • Having things to do together with their own friends.
 

Ideate

IDEATEStructure Diagram

Another way to help me to further define the core concept and visualise the overall process is through the structure diagram. Along with the Problem Statement, it allows me to communicate the fundamental idea.

I used it as a high-level eco-system diagrams as part of my overall visualisation process. These fundamental sketches help me understand the 'moving parts' of Surround•er and also act as key consensus building tools for stakeholders.

Main Features:

  • Find a hunt to play.
  • Find the Team to join with other players.
  • Create a Hunt for others to play.

IDEATEUser Flow

I defined 5 primary flows based on my personas' needs, and map out every steps they will take from starting to complete the task:

  • Find a Hunt
  • Corporate with other players
  • Private Invitation
  • Hunt Play (Solo)
  • Create a Hunt

IDEATESite Map

After defining the user flows, I use a SITE MAP to visualise how the navigation should be structured, identify where the content will sit and show the relationship between different screens and pages.

I kept coming back to refine my site map in the process of the ideation. I refined my site map based on interviews and card sorting exercise with users and minimised the pages and steps for users to complete the goals.

 

Prototype

PROTOTYPEPrototype

Low-Fi Wireframe

The beginning of my wireframe process starts with pen and paper to sketch the LOW-FI WIREFRAME for my main features and highlight only the high-level functionality of a design.

To get my initial thinking out quickly, I start with pen and paper and sketch my ideas. This allows me to efficiently visualise different approaches to the problem. I know I will be refining these concepts as we move forward.

Mid-Fi Wireframe

MID-FI WIREFRAME allows me to refine the look and feel of the app as well as the interaction design.

In this phase I am replacing elements in my sketches with more 'real' content. I begin with the things I know about the design; whether it is text labels or headlines or navigation items. This is where I really begin to flesh out the site hierarchy and function.

Hi-Fi Wireframe

Finally, I use HIGH-FI WIREFRAME that refer to the level of details and functionality built into a prototype. My HIGH-FI WIREFRAMES are ready for prototyping and testing and almost ready to begin the UI design phase.

The more finished my High-Fi wireframes, the better the feedback to my prototype in user testing. Moreover, it helps me to effectively communicate functionality to developers and other stakeholders.

 

Test

TESTUsability Test

I conduct the formal USABILITY TEST with 5 participants to see if the solution meets their needs and if my UX Design is working well.

After conducting the usability test, I used affinity mapping techniques to help visualise the learnings and prioritise the importance of the issues brought to light. This visual approach to organising feedback also helps in communicating with stakeholders.

I then determined which errors are most critical based on a high frequency of reports or a high level severity and created a test report to communicate my findings and propose solutions.

Main Focus:

  • Label then hunt icons on the home page.
  • Emphasis the reward system.
  • Relabel the navigation icons.

TESTRefined Wireframe

Usability test results give me new information to refine my wireframe deck before moving on to the final design. At this point, I am confident any issues or possible friction has been removed or solved.

I take the time to refine my wireframe deck from the usability feedback. I really appreciate the ability to constantly improve the design and concept throughout the process.

Challenge

Mascot Idea is a Failure

At first, the way for users to play the Surround·er is to collect the mascots from different places, but the problems is that:

Firstly, the mascots didn’t catch the users’ eyes, and it would be too similar to Pokemon Go. According to our participant from the first round of the usability testing, if there is no storyline behind these mascots, they won’t consider playing the game and collecting these mascots.

Secondly, users can create their games for other players to play. Here comes the problem: When they are creating their own hunt game, they will go through the process to decide which mascot they want the players to collect in this game. If they can look through our mascots library, which may lose their curiosity and demotivate them to collect the mascots anymore.

Solution

So I changed the mascot’s idea to a more manageable one, which is using virtual items to replace the mascots:

  • Players will receive a task in each of the hunt games. They need to go through different locations to collect these virtual items to win the game.

This way, we can not only make sure these virtual items can have more connections with each hunts’ theme but also make users more focus on the game itself.

What Went Well

User Research

At first, I was nervous about doing the user research and thought it was overwhelming, but after conducting the user interview, I collected so much useful information from them.

Knowing their pain from the existing products, the itch they want to scratch, and the problem they want to solve makes me have more concrete ideas about how Surround·er should be designed.

User Persona

After conducting the very efficient user research, I then created 3 primary personas for my project, Surround·er. It was interesting to create a persona based on the real data. During this process, I make sure I kept their frustrations, motivations, goals and needs in mind every now and then during the whole process.

What Didn't Go Well

UI Design

This is my first time designing UI design and combining every visual design element on the screen with the colour.

At first, my colour combination and the design principle's use didn't come into place and looked dodgy, but I tried to get inspired by other designers by browsing the Dribble, Behance, and Instagram to make me immerse in this field more. As a result, I improve my sense of aesthetics and happy with my output.

What Sould Be Improve

I spent too much time making the wireframe and prototype look pixel perfect. As a result, I was in a rush to do the revision on time. And because of that, when conducting the usability test, it made me biases and started to take any comment personally.

Next time, I will make sure I break project work into time-blocks so as to manage workload better while not compromising on quality. And when conducting the usability test, I will reset the mindset and always be objective because I know in the end, users matters the most.

 

Final Showcase

 

WRAP UP

Digital products are 'living media'. They continue as long as the product is viable and supported. I continuously gather information and look ahead to ensure the product will keep meeting users’ needs.

I applied the iterative design process throughout the entire design project cycle as a means to consistently improve usability. And also make another high-level plan for how I will continue to iterate on the project in the future.

NEW HYPOTHESIS

Problem

Users may play the same hunt only once, how might we engage our users more and make them keep using and playing our application?

Proposal Solutions

Allow users to have more ways to decorate and custom their avatar, plus get various properties for their avatar when they complete the hunts might seem the good way to engage our users more.

Or by adding the milestones or badges for the user so that they can see their journey(progress)
