WORDS

VOCABULARY LEARNING APP

Language learning app can't just have an educational purpose but also a social purpose.

WORDS is the vocabulary learning app that allows you to have your own mother tongue's buddy that making sure to help you gain more confidence in communicating with the locals while traveling or visiting other countries.

OVERVIEW

  • ROLE:
    UX Designer
  • OCCASION:
    School Project
  • DURATION:
    July-August 2019 (4 weeks)
  • METHOD:
    Design Thinking and Lean UX
  • HOW MIGHT WE...
    Empower people to learn new vocabulary?
  • KEY FEATURES:
    Vocal Flashcard, Learning Buddy

- THINK -

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Learning something new is a critical life skill no matter our age, location, or circumstances, especially comes to language learning.

There are so many language learning apps in the marketplace now. By doing the competitor analysis, I’m looking for a pattern that makes the well-known products so successful, but most importantly, spotting the area that may cause the users’ frictions that our product can offer a better solution for the user and fit in the marketplace.

Tinycards

It is pleasantly intuitive to use, although in the beginning, it is a bit confusing, once you get used to it, it can be easy and enjoyable.

I like the way they have so many languages and so many different topics that can be chosen, but I think they can do better for categorising different languages.

Quizlet

I really like the concept which teachers can use it as a game for teaching. And the offline setting can help the learner uses it everywhere without any restriction.

But I hope they can put the menu on as it is a bit difficult for the user to explore the different topics or categories.

VocabuLazy

This app focuses on the students who are going to take the exam, it is easy for them to find the subject they want to learn, but I think they can provide more features to let the user explore more for themselves.

I think this app is still at an early stage comparing to the other two.

USER RESEARCH

To effectively employ the principles of user-centred design, I talk directly to my users at the very beginning of the project to understand how design can meet their needs and requirements.

I conducted user research with 3 participants with the goals in mind:

  • Understanding their motivation of learning new languages.
  • What factors might cause it harder to learn?
  • Understanding their day-to-day life.

- MAKE -

PROTO-PERSONA

After talking to the user, I have a better understanding of the app I’m going to create. I created a personas that based on my research, extensive user and market research.

I also came out with some ideas about what WORDS should be focus on:

  • 10-20 min per session.
  • Focus on communication and speaking.
  • Vocal flashcard for recording.

USER FLOW

I defined 2 tasks for Dale, my personas, would have done while using our product as a new user:

  • Sign up and Choose the topic/language he wants.
  • Create new flashcard and record.

TASK 1:
Sign up and Choose the topic/language he wants.

USER GOAL:
Sign up as the new user, and create their own personalise learning homepage.

Entry Point:
Open the app

Success Criteria:
Let the user log in to their own personalise homepage.

TASK 2:
Create new flashcard and record.

USER GOAL:
Allow users add the new vocabulary and definition or new record on their Flashcard Page.

Entry Point:
Home Page

Success Criteria:
Create the new folder to add the new words or add the new words in the existence folder.

REFINED

I refined the wireframes based on what I learned from the test participants. I then chose 3 primary issues that should be fixed ASAP :

  • When choosing the language, using the flag as a button is not effective and may confuse the user.
  • Participants tried to use the search bar as an embed dictionary.
  • Participants complained that they wanted to choose their buddy rather than dispatching by the app.

# 1 : Choosing Language: Using text instead of the flag.

# 2 : Allowing use search bar to look up the words.

# 3 : Give more autonomous when choosing the buddy. (ie. Rematch the buddy)

See prototype on Prottapp

WRAP UP

It’s my first time using UX and design thinking methods to approach the problem, it’s definitely a learning progress for me as I went through the whole process.

I really enjoyed using pen and paper to sketch the wireframes, especially refining them after talking to the user. Because at that point, I knew that the product is getting better, and I would have more confidence that the product will meet users’ needs.

I would say the most interesting thing I learned from this project is that users won’t always know what they want, so I believe as a UX designer, it is important to find out what users are trying to achieve in a particular circumstance and knowing User's 'Jobs to Be Done'.
